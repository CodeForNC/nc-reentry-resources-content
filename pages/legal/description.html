A person’s specific criminal history can have a tremendous impact on his or her ability to gain employment, get professional licenses, acquire housing, and obtain benefits.

At the same time, there are many myths about what a person may or may not be entitled to if he or she has been convicted of a crime. It is important to know and understand your rights and that laws can change.

The UNC School of Government has created a C-CAT, an online tool to discover specific collateral consequences of criminal convictions.

Expunging a Criminal Conviction

In some cases it is possible to remove (or expunge) prior convictions from a person’s record. The UNC School of Government has prepared an extensive Relief From A Criminal Conviction guide explaining what mechanisms are available under North Carolina Law.
