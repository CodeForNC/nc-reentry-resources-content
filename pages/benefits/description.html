Having a criminal conviction will not necessarily prevent someone from receiving benefits such as Food Stamps, Medicaid, Medicare, Social Security or Social Security Disability income.

A good place to start is the Federal Interagency Reentry Council’s Reentry MythBusters website. It dispels common myths about what federal benefits a person may qualify for, even if convicted of a felony. Another good resource is the Social Security Administration, which provides information on qualifying and applying for benefits.
