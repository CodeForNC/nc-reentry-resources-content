Let’s get a job and get paid! Finding work takes work. There are several resources and programs that can help you prepare — including résumé writing, job search and interviewing tips, and job training. Many of the resources are free.

Programs

  • The NCWorks Career Center and the Former Offender Initiative by the North Carolina Department of Commerce help job seekers who have criminal records overcome barriers to employment.
  • Goodwill Industries International provides training and job services. You can search for a Goodwill in your area by checking the "Job & Career Support" box at the top of the page under the main menu and entering your city or town in the search box.
  • Lionheart.org offers emotional literacy programs that can help better the lives of individuals and families.
  • Bonds4jobs.com is a free government service "safety net" giving employers access to job seekers who may be at a hiring disadvantage.
  • NC Peer Support Specialist Program includes people living in recovery who have a mental illness or substance abuse disorder who provide support to others who can benefit from their lived experiences. They are certified by the State to provide support to others with mental health or substance use disorders.

Career Connections and Job Link Centers

  • The NCWorks Career Centers page is a comprehensive list of the Career Centers in each county.
  • Vocational Rehabilitation Services provides employment assistance to those with a qualifying mental or physical disability.
